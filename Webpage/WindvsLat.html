Wind Speed vs Lattitude. ..

In general, the wind speed increases with height from the surface to the upper troposphere. There are several reasons that explain this tendency. First, especially in the middle latitudes, the pressure gradient increases with height. This is shown in the plot. The height of the troposphere is taller in warmer air since warmer air is less dense and thus occupies a greater volume. Going up in altitude, the pressure gradient between the warm air and the cold air increases with height. This can be noticed for pressure being slanted more as height is increased. A higher slant results in a greater pressure gradient between the warm and cold air and thus stronger wind..
